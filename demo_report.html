What this number means

Weighted distinctiveness (73.5%) is the share of your text corpus that lives in clusters well-separated from their nearest neighbours, as judged by the LLM under your equivalence rule.

A cluster scores high when items from a neighbouring cluster, secretly planted among its members, are reliably detected and isolated. A low score means the cluster bleeds into its neighbours — the LLM cannot tell them apart under your rule.

The weighting is by cluster size, so a large cluster that fails pulls the score down more than a small one that fails.

How it is computed

Step 1 — Calibration. Before measuring anything, the LLM is run on planted controls of known composition: groups of truly-same items (→ measures specificity Sp), mixed groups of items from two different clusters (→ sensitivity Se), nonsense text (→ junk detection), and items from far-away clusters (→ far detection). The gate passes only if the judge is reliable enough on all four checks.

Step 2 — Intruder detection. For each cluster, one item from a neighbouring cluster is planted among k=? home items and the LLM is asked to sort them by kind. If the planted item ends up as its own singleton group it is detected. This is repeated across several waves and neighbour sources.

Step 3 — Correction. Raw detection rates are corrected for the chance isolation rate γ (how often the LLM isolates a truly-same item by accident) using the Rogan–Gladen formula: corrected = (raw − γ) / (1 − γ).

Step 4 — Aggregation. Each cluster's corrected detection rate is thresholded at 50%. The headline number is the size-weighted fraction of clusters above that threshold (raw weighted average score: 82%).

How much to trust it

Trust rests on two things:

Calibration gate: PASS. A PASS means the judge separated known-good from known-bad controls reliably enough for the error correction to be meaningful. A FAIL means the numbers below are unreliable — the judge or the rule needs fixing before you read the scores.

Per-cluster confidence intervals (lo–hi columns in the table below) are 95% Wilson intervals on the corrected detection rate. Clusters with few intruder draws (n) have wide intervals. Raise intruder_waves_max or coverage_target to narrow them.
